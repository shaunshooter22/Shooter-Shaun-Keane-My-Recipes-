Description

Fufu and Palm Nut Soup is a traditional West African meal that is hearty, flavorful, and deeply rooted in the culture of countries like Ghana, Nigeria, and Côte d'Ivoire. Fufu is a soft, stretchy dough-like side made by boiling and pounding starchy foods such as cassava, yam, or plantain (or sometimes a mix). It’s smooth in texture and typically served in round portions. Fufu is not chewed — it’s used to scoop up soups and stews with your fingers. Palm Nut Soup (also called Banga soup in Nigeria) is made from the extract of palm fruit. It's rich, oily, and full of flavor. The soup is usually cooked with meats like goat, beef, or chicken, along with smoked fish, dried fish, and sometimes snails or crab. It’s seasoned with local spices and thickened with ingredients like onions, chili peppers, and traditional seasonings. The combination of fufu and palm nut soup is comforting, filling, and often eaten during lunch or dinner with family. It’s more than just food — it’s a cherished part of West African identity and hospitality.

Ingredients

  1. 2 cups of palm nut concentrate (or palm fruit extract)
  2. 1 kg (about 2.2 lbs) of assorted meats (e.g., goat, beef, or chicken)
  3. 1 cup of smoked fish or dried fish (optional)
  4. 1/2 cup of ground crayfish (optional, for flavor)
  5. 2 tablespoons of ground pepper or blended fresh chili (adjust to taste)
  6. 1 large onion, chopped
  7. 2 seasoning cubes (e.g., Maggi or Knorr)
  8. Salt to taste
  9. Water (enough to adjust soup consistency)
  10. Optional: snails, crab, or periwinkle for extra protein
  11. For the fufu: 2 cups of fufu flour (or boiled yam, cassava, or plantain)
  12. Water for mixing or boiling the fufu

Steps

  1. Wash and season the assorted meats with chopped onions, seasoning cubes, and salt. Boil until tender, then set aside the meat and stock.
  2. If using smoked or dried fish, clean it thoroughly and set it aside.
  3. In a large pot, pour in the palm nut concentrate and add a bit of water to loosen it to a soup-like consistency.
  4. Place the pot over medium heat and bring it to a boil while stirring occasionally.
  5. Add the cooked meat, meat stock, and smoked fish to the boiling palm nut base.
  6. Stir in the ground crayfish (if using), ground pepper or blended chili, and adjust salt and seasoning cubes to taste.
  7. Let the soup simmer uncovered for 20–30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens slightly and the oil begins to rise to the top.
  8. While the soup is cooking, prepare the fufu: boil water and stir in fufu flour gradually, turning and pounding continuously until smooth and stretchy. Add more water or flour as needed to get the right consistency.
  9. Once the soup is ready, remove from heat. Serve hot with portions of fufu shaped into balls.
  10. Enjoy your fufu and palm nut soup with clean hands — no cutlery needed!
